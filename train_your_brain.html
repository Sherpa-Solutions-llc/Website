Pacing & the Power of Silence

High-value communicators convey confidence by refusing to rush. They own their space in the conversation.

  • Slow Down: Deliberate speaking signals that your words hold weight.
  • The Strategic Pause: Use silence before a key point to build anticipation, and after to let it settle.
  • Eliminate Fillers: Replace "um" and "uh" with comfortable silence.

High-Value Vocabulary

Clarity and directness always win. Stripping away weak language instantly elevates your perceived authority.

  • Remove Qualifiers: Drop phrases like "I think maybe" or "I just wanted to."
  • Be Direct: State the problem, then state the solution.
  • Keep it Simple: True experts don't hide behind excessive jargon; they make complex things accessible.

Tonality & Physical Presence

How you deliver the message is just as important as the words themselves. Project a grounded, unwavering energy.

  • Grounded Voice: Breathe from the diaphragm to create a richer, resonant tone instead of a thin, anxious voice.
  • Stillness: Avoid erratic movements, fidgeting, or over-gesturing. Stillness commands respect.
  • Steady Eye Contact: Maintain natural, unwavering eye contact without staring aggressively.
